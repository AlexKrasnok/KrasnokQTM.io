Professional CV

Prof. Aleksandr “Alex” Krasnok

Assistant Professor of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Florida International University and Director of the Quantum Technologies and Metamaterials Lab.

Professional Profile

Prof. Krasnok develops theory, computation, and experiments for quantum photonics, complex-frequency electrodynamics, resonant scattering, metasurfaces, cryogenic quantum systems, and quantum sensing. His work connects fundamental wave physics with practical engineering platforms for quantum hardware, sensing, RF systems, and nanophotonics.

Email: akrasnok@fiu.edu

Research Areas

Current work spans wave physics, quantum hardware interfaces, metamaterial-enabled sensing, and computational electromagnetic design.

Complex-Frequency Electrodynamics

Pole-zero engineering, non-Hermitian scattering, waveform design, Fano interference, virtual absorption, and resonant wave control.

Quantum Technologies

Quantum control, circuit/cavity-QED interfaces, superconducting microwave cavities, qubit diagnostics, and cryogenic photonic links.

Metamaterials and Sensing

Metasurfaces, Mie resonators, NV-diamond magnetometry, MRI field-control metasurfaces, RF sensing, and applied metrology.

Research Support

QTM Lab research has more than $1.4M in awarded external, fellowship, and in-kind support at or through FIU, including DOE MSRC/MEERCAT El-Pho, ONR nanoscale coatings research, NSF I-Corps Meta-MRI, AFOSR complex-frequency plasma research, IC postdoctoral fellowship support, IonQ quantum compute credits, NVIDIA GPU hours, and DOE SBIR-linked work.

Teaching, Mentoring, and Service

Prof. Krasnok teaches quantum engineering and computational electromagnetic topics with reproducible, hands-on workflows.

Teaching

Prof. Krasnok launched FIU's first undergraduate/graduate quantum-computing sequence, EEE 4423 and EEE 6429. Courses use Qiskit, Python, reproducible notebooks, recorded modules, and no-cost materials.

Mentoring

Mentoring emphasizes rigorous modeling, Python/Jupyter workflows, simulation, measurement practice, documentation, publication-quality figures, and technical writing.

Professional Service

Service includes Co-Director leadership for the FIU Quantum Initiative, associate editorship for Photonics Research, editorial board service for Advanced Photonics Nexus and Reviews on Advanced Materials Science, guest editing, and IEEE RAPID session organization.
